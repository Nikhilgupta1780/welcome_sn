Early Life of Lord Buddha

Siddhartha Gautama, later known as Lord Buddha, was born around 563 BCE in Lumbini, Nepal. He was born into a royal family in Kapilvastu as the son of King Suddhodana and Queen Maya. Despite growing up in luxury, he was deeply moved by the suffering of the world when he encountered old age, illness, death, and an ascetic. This realization marked the beginning of his spiritual journey.

Enlightenment

At the age of 29, Siddhartha renounced his royal life and embarked on a quest for spiritual awakening. After years of ascetic practices, he meditated under the Bodhi tree in Bodh Gaya, India. It was here, at the age of 35, that he attained enlightenment and became the Buddha, meaning "The Enlightened One."

Teachings of Lord Buddha

Lord Buddha's teachings are based on compassion, mindfulness, and self-awareness. The core of his philosophy includes:

  • The Four Noble Truths: These truths explain the nature of suffering, its cause, its cessation, and the path to end it.
  • The Eightfold Path: A guide to ethical and mental development to achieve liberation from suffering.
  • Middle Way: A balanced approach to life, avoiding extremes of self-indulgence and self-mortification.

Legacy of Lord Buddha

Buddhism, inspired by Lord Buddha's teachings, has become one of the major world religions with millions of followers globally. Sites like Lumbini, Bodh Gaya, Sarnath, and Kushinagar are significant pilgrimage destinations. His teachings on compassion, non-violence, and mindfulness continue to inspire people across cultures and generations.
